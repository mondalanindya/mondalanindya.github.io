Welcome to my personal website! I am a PhD candidate at the Surrey Institute for People-Centred AI, CVSSP, University of Surrey.

I obtained my Bachelor of Engineering (Hons.) degree in Electronics and Telecommunication from Jadavpur University, India, where I collaborated with some remarkable researchers from institutions like the Indian Institute of Science, La Rochelle Université and Télécom Paris.

My research focus is in the areas of machine learning, computer vision, and the internet of things, with a current emphasis on video representation learning by vision-language modeling. I have also delved into the realms of graph neural networks, graph signal processing, and subspace clustering.

The best way to get in touch with me is by emailing me at anindyam[dot]jan[at]gmail[dot]com. I rarely check my other social media accounts (like LinkedIn, Twitter, and others), so messages sent there may go unnoticed.

Selected Projects

You can find some of the codes I have written on my GitHub account.

  • Reconstructing Time-varying Graph Signals using Graph Neural Networks: Designed a Graph Convolutional Network (GCN) framework for reconstructing time-varying graph signals from their samples. [Findings reported at ICASSP]

  • Source-free Domain Adaptation for Image Classification: Developed a source-free domain adaptation (SFDA) algorithm by exploiting class-level consistency for image classification. [Findings reported at internship report]

  • Recovery of Missing Data in IoT Sensor Networks using Graph Signal Processing: Applied a Sobolev norm minimization method for reconstructing time-varying graph signals to recover missing data in sensor networks. [Findings reported at EUSIPCO]

  • Graph Signal Processing for Event-based Vision:Developed a graph-based semi-supervised learning algorithm for semantic segmentation in event-based data. Also applied a modification of Graph Spectral Clustering to detect moving objects from event-data. [Findings reported at ICCVW]

Anindya Mondal

Hi there. You have landed on Anindya's webpage. I am postgraduate research student at the Surrey Institute for People-Centred AI, CVSSP, University of Surrey.

I obtained my Bachelor of Engineering (Hons.) degree in Electronics and Telecommunication from Jadavpur University, India. During my undergrad, I had the pleasure of working with some amazing researchers from the Indian Institute of Science (India), La Rochelle Université (France) and Télécom Paris (France).

My broad research interests are machine learning, computer vision, and the internet of things. Currently, I am working on animal behaviour analysis in a multimodal setting (primarily vision-language modelling). Previously I have worked in graph neural networks, graph signal processing and subspace clustering.

Research Projects Undertaken

  • Animal Action Recognition via Vision-Language Multimodal Learning: Formulating the problem of animal action recognition as a vision-language multimodal learning problem.

  • Reconstructing Time-varying Graph Signals using Graph Neural Networks: Worked on designing a Graph Convolutional Network (GCN) framework for reconstructing time-varying graph signals from their samples.

  • Source-free Domain Adaptation for Object Classification: Worked on designing a source-free domain adaptation (SFDA) algorithm by exploiting class-level consistency for object classification.

  • Recovery of Missing Data in IoT Sensor Networks using Graph Signal Processing: Applied a Sobolev norm minimization method for reconstructing time-varying graph signals to recover missing data in sensor networks. Our approach improved upon the previous works by as much as 54%.

  • Graph Signal Processing for Event-based Vision:Worked on developing a graph-based semi-supervised learning algorithm for semantic segmentation in event-based data. Furthermore, applied a modification of Graph Spectral Clustering to detect moving objects from event-data.

Welcome to my personal website!

I obtained my Bachelor of Engineering (Hons.) degree in Electronics and Telecommunication from Jadavpur University, India, where I collaborated with some remarkable researchers from institutions like the Indian Institute of Science, La Rochelle Université and Télécom Paris.

My research focus is in the areas of machine learning, computer vision, and the internet of things, with a current emphasis on video representation learning by vision-language modeling. I have also delved into the realms of graph neural networks, graph signal processing, and subspace clustering.

Research Projects Undertaken

You can find some of the codes I have written on my GitHub account.

  • Reconstructing Time-varying Graph Signals using Graph Neural Networks: Worked on designing a Graph Convolutional Network (GCN) framework for reconstructing time-varying graph signals from their samples.

  • Source-free Domain Adaptation for Image Classification: Worked on designing a source-free domain adaptation (SFDA) algorithm by exploiting class-level consistency for image classification.

  • Recovery of Missing Data in IoT Sensor Networks using Graph Signal Processing: Applied a Sobolev norm minimization method for reconstructing time-varying graph signals to recover missing data in sensor networks. Our approach improved upon the previous works by as much as 54%.

  • Graph Signal Processing for Event-based Vision:Worked on developing a graph-based semi-supervised learning algorithm for semantic segmentation in event-based data. Furthermore, applied a modification of Graph Spectral Clustering to detect moving objects from event-data.
